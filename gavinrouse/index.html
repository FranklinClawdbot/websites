The Story

Not to change the world.
To be a lighthouse in it.

My first real job was driving wheat truck on a farm in eastern Washington. I worked yogurt shops and pizza joints. I quit high school sports and enrolled in Running Start — not because I couldn't hack it, but because I wanted to work, make money, and learn things the classroom wasn't teaching.

I got a CS degree, landed at a medical device company, and started building software quality systems for neurodiagnostic equipment used in hospitals worldwide. I was good at it. I grew from intern to team lead, became the longest-tenured member of a growing quality team, and shipped 30+ releases of software that monitors the electrical activity of the human brain. It was meaningful work. It still is.

Then came 2020. The hardest years of my life cracked me open and rebuilt me. I found God. I left a toxic relationship. Everything about the trajectory of my life changed. I came out the other side a different man — the same in a lot of ways, but fundamentally reoriented toward what actually matters.

What followed wasn't a plan. It was what happens when a man with conviction and curiosity starts saying yes. I picked up a camera and built a videography business — 150K views on YouTube, 15+ events, 7 weddings, nearly $10K in revenue. I met and married my wife and started building toward a family. I stepped into political service as treasurer of my county's Republican party. I discovered AI and realized one person, armed with the right tools and the right principles, could build at a scale that used to require a team.

None of it would have happened without the fire.
